About

More About Me

For the better part of the past year, I've been working as a Marketing Intern at TransUnion Canada in Burlington. I am also in my 3rd year of the Business Administration program at Wilfrid Laurier University working toward a specialization in Marketing and a minor in Psychology. I thrive in environments where I'm constantly challenged to learn, adapt and innovate.

Why I chose marketing

Growing up we are often asked "what do you want to be when you grow up?" - this question is easy to answer when you're five (a princess, obviously) but becomes increasingly more difficult as we get older.

When it came time for me to decide what path to follow in my studies, instead of asking myself what I wanted to be, I asked myself the following:

"What am I good at?"

"What am I passionate about?"

I've always had an interest in words. English was one of my favourite subjects in high school and often carried a book in my purse. I knew that whatever job I had it had to involve words - communicating by crafting language. The chance to think outside the box and create.

On the other hand, I also excelled in math. I am a walking paradox - loving the creativity and freedom of words while still appreciating the rules and patterns of numbers. I'm fascinated by trends and data. I like to organize, plan, and strategize.

When I realized that marketing was a natural combination of these two worlds - structured yet chaotic - I knew it was something I needed to explore. Being fortunate enough to be hired as a part of the Marketing team at TransUnion, I've been exposed to several aspects of the field over the past year - content creation, sales enablement, video production, campaigns, lead generation, social media marketing and more.
